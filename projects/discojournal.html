DISCOJOURNAL

DiSCo Journal is an annual online publication that explores digital art, culture, and methodologies, serving as a critical space to question the meaning of digitalization in everyday life. It aims to foster interdisciplinary conversations and insights into the impact of technology on society. For this project, I was responsible for the branding of the collective, creating a visual identity that reflects its interdisciplinary and critical approach.

I also designed and developed the website, ensuring it provides an engaging user experience. Additionally, I conceptualized and designed the first two issues of the journal, each with its own unique focus.

Issue 1 channels DISCO vibes from the 1970s, incorporating playful references to music, culture, and aesthetics of the disco era, while examining their resonance in contemporary digital culture. Issue 2 delves into the realm of digital art, featuring themes like ASCII art, hacking, and the creative possibilities of early digital technologies.

The result is a cohesive brand identity and dynamic online platform that seamlessly integrate the journal's themes, offering readers a visually compelling and thought-provoking experience.
