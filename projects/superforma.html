SOCIAL NETWORKS BY CULTURAL AREAS

This project visualizes the usage of social networks across the globe, analyzing how cultural and geographical contexts shape social media habits. Based on data from the Global Digital Yearbook (2019), it reveals that users in the 44 most socially active countries spend, on average, over 2 hours daily on social networks, far exceeding the global average of 36 minutes. The project was developed as part of the Density Design Final Synthesis Lab.

The visualization categorizes countries into 11 macro-regions inspired by Jerome D. Fellmann’s Human Geography (2008), with Europe further divided into Northern, Southern, and Middle European areas. The visualization categorizes 43 social networks into five families—Blogging, Video Hosting, Messaging, Image Sharing, and Job Networking—and maps their usage across cultural regions.

In this project, I focused on the conceptual framework, UX/UI design, and the design of the visualization, ensuring a clear and engaging user experience. I also played a key role in researching and clustering data to accurately represent social network penetration and its cultural variations. The result is an intuitive and impactful visualization that offers a comprehensive perspective on global social media habits.
